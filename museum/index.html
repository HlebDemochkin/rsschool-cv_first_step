Las Meninas is a 1656 painting by Diego Velazquez, the leading artist of the Spanish Golden Age.

It was cleaned in 1984 to remove a [layer] of dust that had gathered since the previous restoration in the 19th century.

The cleaning provoked furious protests, not because the picture had been damaged in any way, but because it looked different.
